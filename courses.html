Courses

Fall 2014

JAPN 201 Elementary Japanese I

Prerequisite: No Japanese language background

This course is for those with no background of Japanese language: Students with experience learning the language must take a placement test offered in August. Contact EALC Department (244-1432) for more information & sign-up. This course aims at developing ability to communicate with native speakers of Japanese in authentic Japanese both orally and in writing. The focus of this course is your performance in class through speaking and writing Japanese. The course introduces basic vocabulary and structural patterns and simple conversational skills as used in social situations in which you would actually encounter in Japan or on campus. Some basic strategies for reading authentic Japanese materials are also introduced.

JAPN 203 Intermediate Japanese I

Prerequisite: JAPN 202 or equivalent, or as determined by placement test/ instructor
Transfer students and students who wish to take this course after a long absense of Japanese language study must take a placement test.

This course continues to promote ability to communicate with native speakers of Japanese in authentic Japanese while introducing more variety of everyday practical situations in which you would establish smooth communication with native speakers of Japanese. The reading and writing skills will be expanded into a discourse level where you will read / write longer texts as well as functional materials such as directions, thank-you letters and Japanese resumes.

JAPN 305 Advanced Japanese I

Prerequisite: JAPN 204 or by placement test

Reading and Writing Section: The purpose of this course is to train students in academic reading and writing so that they can conduct research in Japanese. The course offers intensive reading and writing practices. Advanced knowledge of Kanji is required.

JAPN 440 Fourth-Year Japanese I

Prerequisite: JAPN 306 or equivalent. 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours.

Further developments of skills in sophisticated Japanese language use, including readings in authentic materials in a wide variety of writing styles, writing for formal occasions, and speaking appropriately according to the situation while using precise vocabulary in correct level of speech.

Spring 2015

JAPN 202 Elementary Japanese II

Prerequisite: JAPN 201 or as determined by a placement test / instructor

The goal of this course is to give you the ability to communicate with native speakers of Japanese in authentic Japanese both orally and in writing. The course focuses on your performance of spoken and written Japanese in class. You will acquire culturally and linguistically appropriate behavior which will enable you to establish smooth communication with native speakers of Japanese. In JAPN201, you studied fundamental grammatical structures of Japanese and practiced simple conversational skills such as introducing yourself or someone else, describing and asking about a location, and talking about your daily activities and your preferences. In this course, you will learn how to make a request or suggestion, order food at a restaurant, talk about family members, and many other things that are very useful in your daily life. You will learn approximately 150 kanji characters. At the end of the semester, you will be able to comfortably handle sentences written in combinations of hiragana, katakana and kanji.
